# Factor Analysis Explained + Application to MindLumi

This slide is showing a **factor analysis decision**: out of 11 possible measured variables or dimensions, the analysis suggests keeping **3 core factors**.

In plain English:  
Instead of treating all 11 items as separate, the data is saying:

> “Most of the meaningful pattern can be explained by 3 larger underlying themes.”

---

## What the slide means

The table lists 11 possible factors.

Each factor has an **eigenvalue**, which tells us how much of the total information or variance that factor explains.

A common rule is the **Kaiser/NFACTOR criterion**, which usually keeps factors with an eigenvalue above 1.

Here, the first three eigenvalues are:

1. **Factor 1 = 5.799992**
2. **Factor 2 = 1.964613**
3. **Factor 3 = 1.102480**

After that:

- Factor 4 = **0.527170**
- remaining factors are also below 1

That is why the slide concludes:

## **3 Factors will be retained**

---

## Why 3 factors matter

The first factor explains about:

**52.73%**

The second adds:

**17.86%**

The third adds:

**10.02%**

Together they explain:

## **80.61% of the total variance**

That is a strong result.

It means the model can simplify 11 variables into 3 meaningful categories without losing much information.
